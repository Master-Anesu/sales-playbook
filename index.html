The playbook

The four stages of every sales conversation. Keep them in your head — the live call walks you through the prompts for each.

1

Introduction

Build rapport and set the tone.

  • Greet warmly — use their name
  • Introduce yourself and Trilogy Care
  • State why you're calling (1–2 sentences)
  • Ask permission to continue with a few questions
5 prompts
2

Discover their needs

Uncover the 3 things that matter most.

  • Ask about their current care situation
  • Identify their biggest challenge right now
  • Understand the impact on their daily life
  • Find out what 'ideal care' looks like for them
  • Clarify their timeline and urgency
6 prompts
3

Present the solution

Match the solution to their top triggers.

  • Recap the 3 triggers back to them
  • Explain how Trilogy Care solves trigger #1
  • Highlight relevant services or packages
  • Check they understand and agree
6 prompts
4

Close the conversation

Secure the next step.

  • Ask a clear closing question
  • Pause — let them respond
  • Handle any objections calmly
  • Confirm agreed next steps and timeline
3 prompts

Question cheat sheet

Every prompt question, grouped by stage. Skim before a call, or keep open in another tab.

1

Opening the call

Build trust quickly and get permission to continue.

  • 1
    “Hi [Name], my name is ___ from the Trilogy Care onboarding team. How are you today?”
  • 2
    “I'm calling because [referral source / reason]. I'd love to see how we can help.”
  • 3
    “Before we get started, can I ask — are you looking into this for yourself, or for a family member?”
  • 4
    “What prompted you to look into home care support at this stage?”
  • 5
    “Have you had any experience with home care services before?”
Tip: Mirror their energy. If they sound tired or stressed, slow down and soften your tone. If they're upbeat, match it.
2

Discovering their needs

Understand their situation and uncover the 3 key triggers.

  • 1
    “Do you currently have a Package, or are you waiting for one?”
  • 2
    “What's the biggest challenge you (or your loved one) are facing right now?”
  • 3
    “How is that affecting your quality of life or independence?”
  • 4
    “What does 'good care' look like to you? If everything went perfectly, what would change?”
  • 5
    “Are you receiving any other services at the moment?”
  • 6
    “How soon are you hoping to get support in place?”
Tip: Listen more than you talk here. Write down their exact words — you'll use them when presenting the solution.
3

Presenting the solution

Show them you listened and connect Trilogy Care to what matters most.

  • 1
    “So if I've understood correctly, the three things that matter most to you are [trigger 1], [trigger 2], and [trigger 3] — is that right?”
  • 2
    “Let me explain how we help with [trigger #1] specifically…”
  • 3
    “One thing that makes Trilogy Care different is [key differentiator]. Would that be helpful for you?”
  • 4
    “We have clients in similar situations who've found that [relevant service] made a real difference.”
  • 5
    “Does this sound like the kind of support you've been looking for?”
  • 6
    “Is there anything I've missed, or something else that's important to you?”
Tip: Repeat their own words back to them — it shows you were genuinely listening, not running a script.
4

Closing the conversation

Secure a clear next step — sign-up, home visit, or follow-up call.

  • 1
    “Based on everything we've discussed, I think we can really help. Would you like to get started?”
  • 2
    “The next step to onboarding is for me to take your details down. I'll set up your profile and book in the care plan meeting.”
  • 3
    “Is there anyone else involved in this decision — a son, daughter, or partner — who'd like to be part of the conversation?”
Tip: Don't rush the close. Ask, then pause. Silence is your friend — let them process and respond in their own time.

Call scripts

Word-for-word scripts you can follow on a live call. Read the teal lines out loud, follow the grey action cues, and listen for the amber triggers. Adapt the words to sound natural — these are a guide, not a robot script.

1

Opening the call

Build rapport and get permission to continue · ~2 min

Say
Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Trilogy Care. How are you going today?
Say
Thanks for taking my call. I’m reaching out because [referral source / reason they enquired]. I’d love to have a quick chat about how we might be able to help — does that sound okay?
Say
Before we dive in — can I ask, are you looking into this for yourself, or for a family member?
Say
And what was it that prompted you to start looking into home care support at this point?
Listen for
Their first trigger. Write down their exact words — you’ll feed these back in Stage 3.
Say
Have you had any experience with home care services before — either with another provider or through the government?
If yes
Ask: What worked well, and what didn’t? — this often reveals their biggest pain point.
If no
Say: No worries at all — that’s completely normal. I’ll walk you through how it all works.
Transition to discovery
Say
That’s really helpful, thank you. I’d love to ask a few more questions so I can understand exactly what would work best for you.
2

Discovering their needs

Uncover the 3 key triggers that matter most · ~5 min

Say
Do you currently have a Home Care Package, or are you still waiting for one to come through?
Say
What would you say is the biggest challenge you’re facing right now with day-to-day care?
Listen for
Value bucket keywords: cost, fees, afford = Price · choose, pick, my own carer = Choice · stay at home, independence, routine = Independence. Write down their exact words.
Say
And how is that affecting your quality of life — or your independence at home?
Say
If everything could go perfectly from here, what would good care actually look like for you?
Listen for
This is the gold question. Let them talk. Their answer often reveals their primary driver — don’t interrupt.
Say
Are you receiving any other services at the moment — from another provider, or through the Commonwealth Home Support Programme?
Say
And how soon are you hoping to get support in place? Is there any urgency?
Transition to solution
Say
Okay, I think I’ve got a really clear picture of what matters to you. Let me tell you how we can help with exactly what you’ve described.
3

Presenting the solution

Match Trilogy Care to their top triggers · ~5 min

Say
So if I’ve understood you correctly, the three things that matter most are: [trigger 1], [trigger 2], and [trigger 3]. Have I got that right?
Say
Let me start with [trigger #1], because that seemed like the most important one for you…
Pick the line that matches their primary driver
$ Price: One thing that sets us apart is that we have some of the lowest care management fees in Australia. That means more of your package goes towards actual care — not admin and overheads. On an average package, our clients typically free up extra hours of care per fortnight just by switching.
✦ Choice: With Trilogy Care, you get to pick your own carers. We don’t just send whoever’s available — you build a team of people you actually know and trust. You also choose which services go into your plan and can change them whenever you need to.
⌂ Independence: Our whole model is designed to keep you comfortable and in control at home. You decide what services you need, when you need them, and who provides them. We work around your routine, not the other way around.
Say
We’ve had a lot of clients in similar situations who found that [relevant service] made a real difference. It’s something we hear often.
Additional proof points — use 1 or 2 that fit
$ Price: There are no exit fees, no hidden admin charges, no daily fees. You only pay for care that’s actually delivered.
✦ Choice: We support self-managed care, which means you stay in the driver’s seat but we handle the paperwork and compliance behind the scenes.
⌂ Independence: Families tell us the biggest relief is knowing Mum or Dad can stay home safely, without losing their independence or dignity.
Say
Does that sound like the kind of support you’ve been looking for?
If hesitant
Is there anything else that’s important to you that I haven’t covered? I want to make sure we’re on the same page.
Transition to close
Say
Great. Well, I think we’d be a really good fit for what you need. Let me tell you what the next step looks like.
4

Closing the conversation

Secure the next step — keep it simple and confident · ~2 min

Say
Based on everything we’ve talked about, I genuinely think we can help. Would you like to get the ball rolling?
If yes
Wonderful. The next step is really simple — I’ll just take down a few details, set up your profile, and book in your care plan meeting. It usually takes about five minutes.
If hesitant
Totally understand. There’s no pressure at all. Would it help if I sent through some information you can look over, and we have another chat in a few days?
If they need to talk to family
Of course. Would it help if I gave you a quick summary you can share with them? I’m also happy to jump on a call with everyone together.
Say
Is there anyone else involved in this decision — a son, daughter, or partner — who’d like to be part of the conversation?
Wrap up
Say
Thank you so much for your time today, [Name]. I’ll [confirmed next step] and you’ll hear from us by [timeframe]. If any questions come up before then, you can call us any time.
Remember: These scripts are a guide. The best calls sound like a conversation, not a checklist. Use their name, mirror their words, and let silences do the heavy lifting.
